  • Indian Constitution abolished untouchability (1950)
  • Reservation system introduced for Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and Other Backward Classes (OBC)
  • Social mobility increased, but caste identity remains influential in politics, marriage, and community life

7. Economic and Cultural Impact

  • Caste shaped occupations, land ownership, and trade
  • Influenced rituals, festivals, and family structures
  • Modern urbanization and education have weakened rigid caste norms, but rural areas often retain traditional patterns

8. Myths vs Reality

  • Myth: Caste system was always rigid and oppressive
  • Reality: Early caste was flexible, occupational, and sometimes merit-based
  • Myth: All Indian society followed the same caste rules
  • Reality: There were regional, religious, and temporal variations

9. Challenges Today

  • Discrimination and social prejudice still exist
  • Politics often reinforces caste identities
  • Education, inter-caste marriages, and social reforms are slowly changing perceptions

10. Conclusion: A 3000-Year Journey of Social Hierarchy

The caste system in India has been dynamic, evolving, and contested:

  • From Vedic fluidity to colonial rigidity
  • From social duty to legal reforms and reservations
  • From a community-based identity to a factor in modern politics and society
